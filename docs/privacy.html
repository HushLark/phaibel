Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 22, 2026

Overview

Phaibel is a personal assistant platform. Your data stays under your control. We do not operate central servers that store your personal information. This policy covers the phaibel.com marketing website and the Phaibel iOS app.

The Phaibel iOS App

The Phaibel app connects to a Phaibel server that you own and control — either running on your own computer or a server you host. All your data (tasks, events, notes, contacts) is stored on that server, not by us.

Data the app sends to your server

  • Your typed and spoken messages to the assistant
  • Commands to create, update, or delete items in your vault

Data stored only on your device

  • Your server's URL and connection credentials (stored in the device's secure keychain via iOS Secure Store — never transmitted to us)
  • App preferences and settings

Microphone

Phaibel requests microphone access solely to support voice input. Audio is recorded only while you are actively holding the microphone button. Audio is processed locally by iOS speech recognition or sent to your own server for transcription — it is never sent to Phaibel or any third party we control.

Notifications

Phaibel may send local notifications (reminders, task due dates) delivered entirely on-device. We do not use push notification services that transmit your data to external servers.

LLM providers

Your Phaibel server routes AI requests to providers you configure (such as Anthropic or OpenAI) using API keys you supply. These requests travel from your server directly to the provider — Phaibel does not proxy or log them. Please review your chosen provider's privacy policy.

No account required

The Phaibel app does not require you to create an account with us. There is no sign-up, no email address collected, and no profile stored by Phaibel.

Data deletion

Because all your data lives on your own server, you can delete it at any time by removing files from that server. Uninstalling the app removes all locally stored credentials and preferences from your device.

What We Collect on This Website

When you visit phaibel.com and accept cookies, we use Google Analytics to collect:

  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Referring website
  • Browser type, screen size, and operating system
  • Approximate geographic region (country/state level)

This data is aggregated and anonymous. We do not collect names, email addresses, or any other personally identifiable information through this website.

Cookies

This website uses a single analytics cookie (Google Analytics) which is only loaded after you click "Accept" on the cookie consent banner. If you decline or do not interact with the banner, no tracking cookies are set.

We also store your cookie preference in your browser's localStorage (key: cookie-consent) so we don't ask you again. This is not a cookie and contains no personal data.

Global Privacy Control

We honor the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal. If your browser sends a GPC signal, we treat it as an opt-out and will not load analytics, even if you previously accepted cookies.

Your Rights Under CCPA

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

  • Know what personal information is collected — see "What We Collect" above
  • Delete your personal information — since we only collect anonymous analytics, there is no personal data to delete
  • Opt out of the sale of personal information — we do not sell personal information. The "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" link in our footer revokes analytics consent and re-shows the cookie banner
  • Non-discrimination — we will not treat you differently for exercising your privacy rights

The Phaibel Desktop / CLI Application

The Phaibel CLI and desktop daemon run entirely on your local machine. Your vault data (tasks, events, notes, etc.) is stored as plain Markdown files in a directory you choose. We have no access to this data.

When you configure an LLM provider (OpenAI, Anthropic), your requests are sent directly from your machine to that provider using your own API key. Phaibel does not proxy, log, or intercept these requests. Please review the privacy policies of your chosen LLM provider.

API keys are stored locally in ~/.phaibel/secrets.json and never transmitted to us.
